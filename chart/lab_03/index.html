 Question 3

What are the ratios of arrests to complaints for each neighborhood? Which neighborhoods have ratios that are three times higher than the average?

The average ratio of arrests to compliants in neighborhoods in NYC is 0.14, which means that in average in NYC neighborhoods there are 0.14 complaints for each arrest in NYC.

There are only two neighborhoods with the ratios that are three times higher than the average: West Brighton and Springfield Gardens North.

This means that in these neighborhoods higher than the number of arrests is closer to the number of complaints than in the other neighborhoods: For each arrest in West Brighton there is 1.397 complaints and for each arrest in Springfield Gardens North there is 0.749 complaints.
